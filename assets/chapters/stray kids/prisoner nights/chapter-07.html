The darkness.

Now that the neon lights are finally off, Felix realizes for the first time how comforting it is, how necessary it is. Human beings, so used to having full control over their surroundings, often overlook how this free will positively affects the rest of their bodies. Felix has learned this the hard way, enduring the glare of artificial light for days on end, with no shadow, no peace for his eyes, no quiet for his mind.

But compliance had brought him the darkness. And then, once again, compliance had brought him a pillow to rest his head on, pajamas to cover his body, and a blanket to keep him warm.

 

Yet these things were nothing compared to what he eventually obtained: a weapon.

And the best part? Hyunjin hadn’t even realized he’d handed it to him.

 

“Enjoy the fried chicken and beer,” his jailer had said, leaving him alone.

 

Felix had filled his stomach and then found himself with his leftovers in hand. Suddenly, with nothing else, he realized that those scraps were the only resources he possessed. So, his mind started thinking, and thinking, and thinking.

 

At first, the idea was to break the can and use the sharp edge as a weapon, but it was too thin, too flexible. Then, he realized that the chicken bone could be easily shaped. It was small enough to hide in his pajama sleeve, hard enough not to break easily, but soft enough to be sharpened. So, in the silence of his cell, Felix spent hours and hours pressing the bone against his palm as he ground it against the rough floor.

 

And in the end, he succeeded in sharpening it into a weapon.

 

That chicken bone had ceased to be waste and had transformed into an opportunity. Now, Felix keeps that opportunity hidden within the padding of his pillow. He knows he must resist the impulse to use this makeshift weapon at the first chance because it’s a card he can only play once. It has to be the perfect moment—so ideal that it will make his escape, if not easy, at least feasible.

 

“Tomorrow is going to be a tough day for you.”

 

Those words echo in Felix’s mind like a dark omen. He’s not ready. Whatever awaits him, he wishes it were far away, distanced by years from today. Although Felix doesn’t know what time it is or how long until tomorrow, he’s well aware that every breath brings him closer to that moment. So, almost instinctively, he tries to slow his breathing as if doing so could delay the inevitable march of time.

 

At some point, he falls asleep.

 

He slips into a calm, soothing dream. He’s back in his small apartment on the outskirts of Seoul. The kettle is hissing, steam rising from its spout. He tears open a pack of instant ramyeon, something cheap he bought at the local supermarket. It’s junk food, full of chemicals — he should eat better — but with his expenses piling up, the ramyeon fills him enough to forget he's eating nothing but garbage.

 

Later, he’s lounging on the couch. A drama plays on the TV, night has fallen. He’s about to drift off, suddenly overwhelmed by the fatigue of the day. He turns off the television, and the little red indicator light glows like a watching eye in the dark. Felix stretches when a sound draws his attention — footsteps.

 

They come from the hallway outside his apartment. He moves to the door, pressing his eye against the peephole. Someone is there. Their hood is pulled low, and they’re wearing a dark mask. The figure leans closer, and all Felix can see through the peephole is their iris. The eye in his dream swells, becoming so enormous it swallows him whole. The eye watches him. The eye sees him.

 

Felix jerks awake, his heart racing in his chest.

The lights are on. Hyunjin stands on the other side of the cage.

 

"Nightmares?"

 

Felix needs a moment to answer. His mind is still rattled by the dream, which is absurd—there’s no way that dream could be more dangerous than the reality he’s in, or that the mysterious figure on the other side of the door had worse intentions than Hyunjin.

 

Felix pulls his knees to his chest, resting his forehead against them, breathing slowly. He’s still in the cage. Tomorrow has already arrived.

 

“What is this place?” Felix asks suddenly.

 

Hyunjin seems surprised by the question. He crouches down, pulling a tripod and a GoPro out of a canvas bag he brought with him.

 

“It’s a cell.”

 

“You know that’s not the answer to the question I asked.”

 

“You asked what this place is, and I answered. It’s a cell. What else do you think it could be? A palace? A train station?”

 

“Don’t bullshit me,” Felix growls, lifting his gaze, sharp. “I’ve been here for over a month. I have the right to know where I am and why.”

 

Hyunjin calmly sets up the tripod and screws the GoPro into place.

 

“You have the *right* to know? You think you’re in a position to claim any rights?”

 

“You’re going to keep me in the dark forever?” Felix asks, his voice tight.

 

“Woke up chatty today, didn’t you?”

No, of course not. If he had a choice, Felix wouldn’t speak a word to that guy. But it just so happens Hyunjin is the only person with the answers to his questions—and the only person he can have any conversation with. Still, Felix realizes he won’t get much out of Hyunjin today. And probably, Hyunjin will never be fully willing to explain what Felix is experiencing or why.

 

“There’s going to be another live stream today,” Hyunjin says after a while.

 

Felix expected it. He knew it was coming, but tried to suppress the thought until the last moment. It’s degrading, humiliating, and he has no idea what kind of long-term consequences this will have once he gets out. What gets posted on the internet stays there forever, right? That problem, though, seems distant. After all, he’s not even sure he’ll ever leave this cell. Who knows if he’ll ever see the blue sky again or feel the warmth of the sun on his skin. Things he’s taken for granted his whole life, and now he craves.

 

“Did you bring the drugs?” Felix asks.

 

“I did.”

 

Only then does Felix release the blanket he’d been clutching around himself like a mantle and moves closer to the bars. Today, Hyunjin has his hair tied in a low ponytail, with a few strands framing his face. The kind of guy Felix could have fallen in love with if they’d met under different circumstances. It’s terrifying to think about. It’s terrifying to think that outside of this cell, that person has a life. Maybe he even has a girlfriend, oblivious to the fact that her partner is holding someone captive, torturing and humiliating him for money.

Under different circumstances, Felix could have fallen for someone like him. It’s terrifying to even think about it. It’s terrifying to realize that, outside this cell, this person has a life. “I have so many questions in my head. It would be nice to get answers to at least a fraction of them,” Felix sighs, placing the pill on his tongue. Last time, the drug came in liquid form, but now it’s a pill. Maybe it’s something different. Not knowing what he’s taking unnerves him, but once again, nothing seems worse than the situation he’s already in.

 

“I told you—if you behave, you’ll be rewarded. The better you behave, the kinder I’ll be to you.”

 

“These live streams, are they part of being ‘kind’ to me?”

 

“They’re necessary.”

 

“Necessary for who?”

 

“Necessary. That’s all,” Hyunjin replies curtly.

 

Felix begins to feel the drug clouding his mind. He stumbles forward, his hands gripping the bars for support. “Ugh—strong,” he mumbles.

 

“Yes. The request today is... specific, and though I was hesitant, I decided to increase the dosage. You probably won’t remember anything that happens.”

 

“No—” Felix squeaks, his inhibitions slowly crumbling. His thoughts spill out as they come. “I don’t want to forget—I want to know. I want to know everything.”

 

Hyunjin sighs, skeptical. He pulls the key from his pocket and slowly approaches the cell door. “You don’t know what you’re saying. Sometimes forgetting makes things easier. Like your first days here. Would you really want to remember those?”

 

“They’re my memories!” Felix protests. The door opens, and Hyunjin steps into the cage. Felix’s head spins so violently, he’s sure he’d collapse if he let go of the bars. “They’re mine—you have no right to take them away from me. Good or bad—I need my memories to understand.”

 

“Understanding is overrated,” Hyunjin replies. His hands cup Felix’s face, lifting it so their eyes meet. “What’s the point of understanding? Would it give you freedom? Would it make you happy? Sometimes, people are happier staying ignorant.”

 

“Understanding would give me hope,” Felix breathes, his hands now gripping Hyunjin’s forearms for support. He clings to him. But Hyunjin’s expression hardens.

 

“Hope? Let it go.”

 

“You can’t take it from me.”

 

“Don’t waste your time hoping to escape this place. What’s the point? There’s no one waiting for you out there.”

 

“I don’t need someone waiting for me to hope for freedom.”

 

Hyunjin rolls his eyes. “The drug is taking too long to kick in. See why it’s better to lower the dose or stop it entirely? You’re still too lucid.”

 

“Who cares? Lucid or not... I still don’t have a choice.”

 

“That’s true, but today, I need you docile enough not to resist.”

 

Felix laughs, feeling drunk, like he’s downed an entire bottle of wine. “Docile? What are you planning to do, you twisted pervert?” he chuckles, as Hyunjin runs a hand through his hair, pulling his head back slightly. Felix is beautiful. Like a wild animal suddenly caged, his resistance is part of his allure. How long will it take to break him? Maybe a lifetime wouldn’t be enough—or maybe he’s closer to breaking than Hyunjin thinks.

 

Hyunjin presses his nose against Felix’s cheek. Felix’s hands move to grip Hyunjin’s shoulders. “Today, the clients voted for a very special reward.”

 

“What kind of reward?” Felix mutters.

 

“The reward of watching you act... the reward of seeing you lose another fragment of innocence.”

 

“I’m not innocent at all,” Felix frowns.

 

“You’ve never been with a man before. That alone makes you innocent in their eyes.”

 

Felix says nothing. Hyunjin’s breath is calm, almost comforting, and it starts to feel like the sweetest, most seductive thing in the world to Felix. He wants to be consumed by it. He wants to feel it all around him. His eyes flutter shut as he sways forward.

Felix laughs, completely devoid of any negative thoughts, free from worry. He feels so good that he wishes the drug's effects would never end.
